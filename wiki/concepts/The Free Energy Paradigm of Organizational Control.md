# The Free Energy Paradigm of Organizational Control

The ultimate synthesis of the 4 papers. Organizations are dissipative structures attempting to minimize surprise (free energy) among their agents.

## Synthesis
When organizations apply rigid restrictive constraints (high external precision) to force performance [[Paper - Meyer 2009]], they override the agent's internal priors [[Paper - Judge 2015]]. However, if this overrides a strong biological prior, the resulting prediction error manifests as acute thermodynamic friction (negative affect) which displaces into CWB [[Paper - Dalal 2020]]. Furthermore, when work becomes boundaryless, the sudden loss of external precision causes a cognitive state-space explosion, leading to metabolic strain unless compensated by purely informational (not restrictive) precision [[Paper - Calderwood 2023]].
