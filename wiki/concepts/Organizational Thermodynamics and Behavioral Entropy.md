# Organizational Thermodynamics and Behavioral Entropy

This concept node aggregates theories treating human organizational behavior not as psychological choices, but as energetic states bound by thermodynamic laws.

## Core Mechanisms
1. **Conservation of Behavioral Energy**: Just as energy cannot be destroyed, behavioral predispositions (entropy) suppressed by strict managerial constraints do not disappear; they are converted into "psychological heat" (negative affect) and displaced into orthogonal dimensions (e.g., Counterproductive Work Behavior). See [[Paper - Dalal 2020]].
2. **Cognitive State Space Expansion**: Extra-normative work (like sudden telework) exponentially increases the cognitive state space. Navigating this expanded space without external "informational cooling" (clarity) requires massive metabolic expenditure, resulting in entropic strain (burnout). See [[Paper - Calderwood 2023]].
