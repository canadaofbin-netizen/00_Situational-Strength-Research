# Concept - Job Insecurity

## Definition
Job insecurity is defined as a subjective appraisal or the perceived threat to the continuity and stability of employment as it is currently experienced.

## Key Themes
- **Technological Threat:** In the modern workplace, job insecurity is often triggered by exposure (physical or psychological) to embodied robots and automation technologies.
- **Maladaptive Behaviors:** Feelings of job insecurity can lead to negative workplace outcomes such as burnout and workplace incivility.
- **Interventions:** Psychological interventions, such as self-affirmation, can buffer the negative effects of job insecurity by helping individuals realize their self-worth is not solely dependent on the evaluative implications of their immediate work environment or the presence of robots.
