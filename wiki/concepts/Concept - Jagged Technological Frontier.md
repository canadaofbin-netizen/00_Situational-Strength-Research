# Jagged Technological Frontier

The "Jagged Technological Frontier" describes the uneven and poorly understood boundary of current Artificial Intelligence (AI) capabilities, particularly concerning generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-4.

## Core Idea
Instead of a straight, predictable line where AI is either capable or incapable of tasks based on a simple continuum of difficulty, the frontier is "jagged". This means that AI might easily handle a complex task (falling *inside* the frontier) while completely failing at a seemingly similar or even simpler task (falling *outside* the frontier).

## Implications for Knowledge Work
As demonstrated by [[Source - Dell'Acqua (2026)]], this jagged frontier has significant implications for knowledge workers:
- **Augmentation within the frontier:** When tasks fall inside the frontier, AI acts as a powerful augmenting force, significantly increasing productivity and quality.
- **Degradation outside the frontier:** When tasks fall outside the frontier, reliance on AI can lead to severe performance degradation, as users may blindly trust incorrect or low-quality AI outputs (often referred to as AI "hallucinations" or logical failures).

## Related Concepts
- This concept challenges traditional views of technology adoption and requires workers to develop intuition about where the jagged frontier lies.
