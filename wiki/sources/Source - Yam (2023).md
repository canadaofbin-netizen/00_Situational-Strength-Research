# Source - Yam (2023)

## Bibliographic Information
- **Title:** The Rise of Robots Increases Job Insecurity and Maladaptive Workplace Behaviors
- **Authors:** Kai Chi Yam, Runkun Su, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Journal:** Journal of Applied Psychology (Vol. 108, No. 5, 850–870)

## Summary
The authors investigate the work-related psychological and behavioral costs associated with exposure to robots in the workplace. Drawing upon the cognitive appraisal theory of stress, they propose that both physical and psychological exposure to robots is positively associated with a heightened sense of job insecurity. Even if an individual's job is not directly threatened by robots, pessimistic societal rhetoric combined with the superiority of robots in narrow tasks can foster feelings of insecurity.

The study predicts and finds that this robot-induced job insecurity leads to maladaptive workplace behaviors, specifically highlighting burnout and workplace incivility. Furthermore, the authors test a psychological intervention—self-affirmation—as a potential buffer to mitigate these negative effects. Self-affirmation helps individuals realize that their self-worth is not solely tied to the immediate evaluative threat posed by robots, thereby reducing both job insecurity and the resulting maladaptive behaviors.
