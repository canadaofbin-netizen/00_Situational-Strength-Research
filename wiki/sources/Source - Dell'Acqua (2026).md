# Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier: Field Experimental Evidence of the Effects of Artificial Intelligence on Knowledge Worker Productivity and Quality

## Bibliographic Information
**Authors:** Fabrizio Dell’Acqua, Edward McFowland III, Ethan Mollick, Hila Lifshitz, Katherine C. Kellogg, Saran Rajendran, Lisa Krayer, François Candelon, Karim R. Lakhani
**Year:** 2026
**Journal:** Organization Science
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1287/orsc.2025.21838

## Summary
The paper introduces the concept of a "jagged technological frontier" to describe the uneven impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI), specifically large language models (LLMs) like GPT-4, on knowledge work. Through a randomized, controlled laboratory-in-the-field experiment with 758 management consultants from Boston Consulting Group (BCG), the study examines how AI assistance affects productivity and quality across different tasks. 

For tasks that fall *within* the frontier of AI capabilities, consultants using AI completed 12.2% more tasks, 25.1% faster, and produced significantly higher quality results than those not using AI. However, for complex tasks that fall *outside* the frontier, subjects using AI were 19% less likely to produce correct solutions compared to the control group. This highlights the uneven, poorly understood boundaries of current AI capabilities, where AI can significantly augment human abilities for some tasks while degrading performance on seemingly similar tasks that lie just beyond the frontier.
